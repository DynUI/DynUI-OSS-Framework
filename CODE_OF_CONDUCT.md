# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want DynUI to be a respectful, useful, and welcoming project for everyone who
participates. We expect contributors, maintainers, and community members to act
with care, curiosity, and professionalism in project spaces.

Project spaces include GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, events,
documentation, chat rooms, and any other forum connected to DynUI.

## Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that helps the community:

- being respectful of different experience levels, backgrounds, identities, and
  viewpoints;
- giving and receiving constructive feedback in good faith;
- keeping technical disagreement focused on the work, not the person;
- making space for others to participate;
- asking clarifying questions before assuming intent;
- acknowledging mistakes and correcting them when possible.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of behavior that is not acceptable:

- harassment, intimidation, stalking, or sustained disruption;
- insulting, demeaning, or discriminatory comments;
- sexualized language, imagery, or attention in project spaces;
- public or private harassment;
- publishing someone else's private information without explicit permission;
- abuse of project processes, including bad-faith reports or repeated off-topic
  disruption;
- any conduct that maintainers reasonably determine is harmful to the project or
  its participants.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to the maintainers
at **EMAIL**. If the issue is also a security or privacy concern,
follow the private reporting path in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

Please include as much context as you can safely share:

- what happened;
- where it happened;
- when it happened;
- who was involved;
- any links, screenshots, or other supporting details.

Reports will be handled as privately as practical. Maintainers will avoid sharing
identifying information beyond what is needed to review and respond to the report.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take action to protect the community and project, including:

- asking for clarification or a change in behavior;
- editing, hiding, or removing comments;
- closing or locking issues, pull requests, or discussions;
- temporarily or permanently limiting participation;
- reporting severe incidents to the relevant platform or authority when required.

Enforcement decisions should be proportionate to the impact and context of the
behavior. People affected by an enforcement action may ask the maintainers to
reconsider by replying to the enforcement notice or contacting the reporting
address above.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies in project spaces and when someone represents DynUI
in public. It also applies outside project spaces when behavior directly affects
the safety, reputation, or participation of the DynUI community.
